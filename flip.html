Flip Studio Guide

This module evaluates a fix-and-flip deal: buy below market, renovate, and sell. It estimates your net profit, return on the cash you put in, and the most you can responsibly pay for the property.

Field Guide

Purchase / Offer Price
What you pay to acquire the property at closing.
After-Repair Value (ARV)
Estimated market value once the rehab is complete — usually from comparable sales.
Expected Sale Price (% of ARV)
What you realistically expect to net at sale, as a percent of ARV. 100% means you expect to hit full ARV.
Holding Period
Months from purchase to sale. Drives carrying costs and converts your ROI to an annualized figure.
Rehab Budget & Contingency
Estimated cost of repairs, plus a percentage buffer for overruns.
Acquisition Costs
Up-front costs to buy: closing, inspection, appraisal, and any other one-time fees.
Financing
Loan amount, annual rate, origination points, and flat lender fees. Leave loan at 0 for an all-cash deal.
Holding Costs
Monthly carrying costs while you own — taxes, insurance, utilities, and other. Income while held is optional.
Selling Costs
Commission and seller-side closing as a percent of the sale price, plus any flat costs.
MAO Rule
The percent of ARV used to back into a maximum offer. 70% is the classic flip rule of thumb.

Glossary

ARV
After-Repair Value — the property's market value once renovations are done.
Net Profit
Expected sale price minus every cost: purchase, rehab, acquisition, holding, and selling, plus any income while held.
ROI
Return on Investment — net profit divided by the cash you actually put in (out of pocket).
Annualized ROI
ROI scaled to a full year, so a 6-month deal can be compared to a 12-month one.
MAO
Maximum Allowable Offer — (ARV × MAO%) − total rehab. The most you can pay and still leave room for profit.
Cash Invested
Your out-of-pocket: down payment (offer minus loan) plus acquisition, rehab, and holding costs. Selling costs come out of the sale proceeds, so they're excluded here.

How It Works

The model works forward from the sale and backward from the offer:

  • Expected sale price is ARV × your expected-sale percentage.
  • Total rehab is your budget plus the contingency percentage.
  • Loan interest is treated as interest-only over the holding period — rate × loan × (months ÷ 12) — which matches how most hard-money and bridge loans carry.
  • Holding costs are the monthly carrying costs times the number of months, plus that loan interest.
  • Net profit subtracts purchase, rehab, acquisition, holding, and selling costs from the expected sale price, then adds any income earned while held.
  • ROI divides net profit by cash invested; annualized ROI scales it to a year.
  • MAO is computed independently as (ARV × MAO%) − total rehab. Compare it to your offer: an offer comfortably below MAO means more cushion.
